Introduction

Hello! I’m Nicole Sylvester, a Computer Science Master’s student at Northern Arizona University. Through my technical career, I’ve immersed myself in projects that blend technology and community— from bioinformatics pipelines at TGen North to web and mobile app development in the CANIS Lab— always striving to create solutions that make an impact. As President of Women Who Compute, I’ve organized events and hackathons to empower women in tech, and I stay involved in volunteering and STEM outreach. When I’m not coding or researching, you’ll find me hiking the trails, cross-stitching, or tinkering with pixel art. Welcome to my space—thanks for stopping by!

Projects

Here are some of my recent projects—showcasing research, development, and creative problem-solving. Feel free to explore!

  • Diversified Anatomy and Physiology Lab Resource App

    Led development of an educational web app for NAU’s BIO 201, collaborating with stakeholders and leveraging Blender to create anatomical models, React, Node.js, Three.js, and Firebase to empower users to create diverse anatomical models. Delegated coding and documentation across a team, ensured timely milestones, and presented the project at Engineering Fest 2023 and the Undergraduate Research Symposium 2024.

  • Resource Resilience Database

    Developed a responsive, accessible web app using Figma, Flutter/Dart, and Firebase to help the Hopi community manage mental health resources with cultural relevance. Additionally, designed and deployed an analytics dashboard integrating multiple data sources from Firebase, providing actionable insights into usage patterns.

  • Isometric Pixel Art Game

    A personal passion project combining pixel art with isometric world-building. Currently in development, this game will feature puzzle-solving, exploration, and a vibrant 2D aesthetic. Check back soon for progress updates!
